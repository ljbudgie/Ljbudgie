## Hi there 👋

Building a sovereign open-source ecosystem around **personal agency**, **human review**, **accessibility**, **privacy-first AI**, and **verifiable proof**.

### Positioning Statement

This work is not a loose set of projects. It is a coherent body of work built around one idea: software should answer to the person using it, not the institution applying pressure to them. Across doctrine, product design, cryptographic proof, accessibility tooling, and AI workflows, the aim is consistent — keep the human mind accountable, keep the facts sovereign, and make technology genuinely useful under real-world conditions.

---

### Why This Work Exists

Modern systems often ask people to trust processes they cannot see, challenge decisions they cannot meaningfully inspect, and surrender data they should never have had to give away. This stack exists to push in the opposite direction.

It is built for:

- **personal agency** — helping people understand, evidence, and act on their own situation
- **trust** — making important flows auditable rather than opaque
- **privacy** — keeping sensitive facts local wherever possible
- **accessibility** — designing for real people, including disability and neurodivergence
- **human-first AI** — using AI as an assistant under accountable human review, not as a substitute for it

---

### Ecosystem / Architecture View

The repositories in this portfolio are best understood as one connected architecture rather than separate experiments.

- [**The Burgess Principle**](https://github.com/ljbudgie/burgess-principle) defines the standard: a binary human-review test for systems that affect real people.
- [**Mirror**](https://github.com/ljbudgie/Mirror-) applies that standard to rights mapping and next-step guidance.
- [**Advocate Companion**](https://github.com/ljbudgie/advocate-companion) extends the same logic into disability and self-advocacy support.
- [**Iris**](https://github.com/ljbudgie/Iris) is the sovereign AI companion that makes the wider system interactive, mobile-first, and voice-accessible.
- [**Iris Gate**](https://github.com/ljbudgie/iris-gate-person) provides the private records and commitment layer behind sensitive workflows.
- [**OpenHear**](https://github.com/ljbudgie/openhear) carries the same sovereignty model into hearing-tech and accessibility.
- [**Nexus AI Hub**](https://github.com/ljbudgie/nexus-ai-hub) is the broader experimentation space for agents, memory, and connected tooling.

What makes the portfolio distinctive is not just the topics, but the consistency of the doctrine across products, implementations, and delivery surfaces.

### Selected Projects

#### [The Burgess Principle](https://github.com/ljbudgie/burgess-principle)

The doctrinal and technical anchor of the ecosystem.

- **What it is:** a binary human-review standard turned into templates, workflows, cryptographic evidence tooling, installable PWA flows, local AI support, sovereign identity, and a verifiable memory ledger.
- **Why it matters:** it gives people a concrete way to test whether meaningful human review actually happened.
- **What principle it expresses:** accountability over automation.

#### [Iris](https://github.com/ljbudgie/Iris)

The sovereign AI companion and most interactive surface in the stack.

- **What it is:** a mobile-first Next.js application with voice interaction, letter templates, Memory Palace workflows, governance visibility, and Person Gate integration.
- **Why it matters:** it turns doctrine into a usable relationship between a person and an AI assistant.
- **What principle it expresses:** AI should assist without displacing human agency.

#### [Mirror](https://github.com/ljbudgie/Mirror-)

The local-first rights mapper.

- **What it is:** a restrained tool that classifies a situation, maps the rights involved, drafts the communication, tracks deadlines, and gives one clear next step.
- **Why it matters:** it reduces confusion at the exact moment people need orientation and structure.
- **What principle it expresses:** clarity, restraint, and local control beat generic chatbot behaviour.

#### [Advocate Companion](https://github.com/ljbudgie/advocate-companion)

The accessibility and reasonable-adjustment layer.

- **What it is:** a self-advocacy tool grounded in disability-aware design and the Burgess Principle.
- **Why it matters:** it treats accessibility as a core systems problem, not a compliance afterthought.
- **What principle it expresses:** dignity and usability should be built in from the start.

#### [Iris Gate](https://github.com/ljbudgie/iris-gate-person)

The sovereign records layer.

- **What it is:** a private vault model for commitments, signed receipts, and fact handling without unnecessary disclosure.
- **Why it matters:** it protects the proof surface behind sensitive personal workflows.
- **What principle it expresses:** reveal the minimum, prove the maximum.

#### [OpenHear](https://github.com/ljbudgie/openhear)

The hearing-sovereignty branch of the ecosystem.

- **What it is:** hearing-tech built around user ownership of audiograms, data, and algorithmic handling.
- **Why it matters:** it extends sovereignty and accessibility into another domain where users are often disempowered.
- **What principle it expresses:** accessibility should preserve user control, not trade it away.

### What Makes This Different

- **It is one doctrine expressed across multiple products** rather than a collection of unrelated demos.
- **It is not trend-chasing** — the through-line is consistent: sovereignty, verification, accessibility, and accountable AI.
- **It joins philosophy to execution** — the ideas are backed by shipped releases, concrete workflows, and real proof surfaces.
- **It treats accessibility as architecture** rather than garnish.
- **It builds AI under constraint** — useful where it helps, bounded where human judgment must remain primary.

That consistency across doctrine, engineering, and delivery is the real differentiator.
